September 26, 2016
Trump's horrendous performance at the first debate signals the beginning of an absurd, scandal-driven downward spiral.

September 27, 2016
Trump continues Alicia Machado attacks at post debate rally, brings up Bill Clinton infidelities.

September 28, 2016
Trump refers to online polls saying he 'won the debate', jokes 'should we keep them?' about non-Christians at rally.

September 29, 2016
Trump's Cuba dealings revealed and embargo violations uncovered. Forbes downgrades Trump's wealth estimate. USA Today, in an historic move, denounces Trump.

September 30, 2016
In a 3 AM tweet, Trump falsely claims that Miss Universe winner appeared in a sex tape. In a completely unsurprising turn of events, it is later revealed that Trump himself appears in the introduction of a softcore porn film.

October 1, 2016
The New York Times releases Trump's 1995 tax returns, which show a $916 million loss. Trump mocks Clinton's pneumonia and claims she hasn't been "loyal to Bill".

October 2, 2016
Fallout from the NYT tax revelations: sources estimate that Trump could have paid no taxes for almost 20 years.

October 3, 2016
Trump calls those with PTSD "weak" while addressing veterans. NY attorney general's office shuts down Trump Foundation, which does not have the proper authorization to solicit charity funds. A Newsweek investigation reveals that Trump bought Chinese-made steel.

October 4, 2016
Trump Foundation political donations uncovered. Wikileaks "October surprise" turns out to be a hoax, leaving the alt-right disappointed.

October 5, 2016
The Atlantic denounces Trump and endorses Clinton. This is only the third endorsement ever in history of paper.

October 6, 2016
Brilliant tactician Trump muses on military action: "Wouldn't it be nice if we attacked first?"

October 7, 2016
Donald "no one respects women more than I do" Trump describes how he grabs women by the genitals without their consent. Donald "law and order candidate" Trump maintains that Central Park Five are guilty, despite DNA evidence.

October 8, 2016: ~*The Saturday Slaughter*~
Donald Trump is uninvited when Paul Ryan excludes him from Wisconsin rally over 'Trump Tape' comments. Unendorsements and denouncements roll in from other Republicans; GOP funds diverted from Trump to down-ticket races.

October 9, 2016: Debate #2
Another disastrous debate for Trump. The Republican siege continues following Trump's off-color pre-debate panel.

October 10, 2016
Paul Ryan tells House Republicans to focus on down-ballot races, saying he won't defend or campaign for Trump. Trump's inaccurate Clinton stories directly linked to Russian state media.

October 11, 2016
The Republican 'Civil War' begins: Trump and supporters fight Ryan and the Republican Party. Top Mormons in "All-Out Revolt" against Trump. Multiple polls show Hillary with a double-digit lead.

October 12, 2016
Multiple women (Jessica Leeds, Rachel Crooks, Mindy McGillivray, Natasha Stoynoff, Jennifer Murphy) accuse Trump of past sexual assault. A 1992 video shows Trump making questionable comments about young girls. Liberty University students rebuke Trump. Australian NSW legislature officially condemns Trump, calling him a "revolting slug, unfit for public office."

October 13, 2016: Trump's Total Meltdown (as described by Time)
Trump demands NYT take down sexual assault allegation article and receives a blistering response. Trump calls the accusations a distraction brought by a conspiracy with Hillary Clinton and a "global power structure" of (((international bankers))). Michelle Obama gives a powerful rebuke of Trump.

October 14, 2016
Two more assault allegations surface: Summer Zervos and Kristin Anderson. Trump, displaying his respect for women, defends against allegations by saying the accusers were too ugly for him.

October 15, 2016
Cathy Heller joins the long list of sexual misconduct accusers. Trump continues conspiratorial defensive, saying Clinton was 'on drugs' at last debate and should take a drug test before the third one. Also claims that the election is being "rigged" by Clinton and the Media -- a notion which is immediately rejected by Paul Ryan. Trump picks fight with swing-state Ohio's Republican Party director.

October 16, 2016
Voters are buying none of Trump's defenses on allegations. Trump intensifies claims that election is 'rigged' in spite of Mike Pence and Paul Ryan saying otherwise. Trump claims that Saturday Night Live is rigging the election as a part of a conspiracy perpetrated by the global media cabal.

October 17, 2016
Trump ups the ante on the debunked claim of widespread voter fraud as Republican allies continue to distance themselves. GOP feud continues as Trump crowd chants "Paul Ryan Sucks" at a rally. Melania Trump claims that sexual assault accusers are lying, and Trump was merely egged on by Billy Bush "to say dirty and bad stuff."

October 18, 2016
Obama tells Trump to "stop whining" about election fraud. People Magazine reveals six witnesses who corroborate Natasha Stoynoff's sexual assault allegations.

October 19, 2016: Debate #3
Trump extends his lead with female voters by calling Clinton a "nasty woman" in the third debate, while maintaining his commitment to not necessarily accept the results of the election.

October 20, 2016: Not even God is laughing
Trump bombs at Al Smith Dinner, somehow losing to Clinton in a charity event.

October 21, 2016
Trump takes a swipe at Michelle Obama, broadening his base by attacking one of the most widely favored political figures in the US. Think Progress reveals that "activist" "filmmaker" James O'Keefe was paid by Trump Foundation.

October 22, 2016
Eleventh Trump accuser - pornstar Jessica Drake - comes forward as Trump says he'll sue his accusers in his first 100 days during his "Gettysburg Address". GOP schism grows as Log Cabin Republicans refuse to support Trump.

October 23, 2016
A rare Trump gaffe break! Kellyanne Conway spends the day ramping up talk of suing Trump accusers, and admits "we are behind". Several Republicans threaten defamation lawsuits for ads associating them with Trump.

October 24, 2016
The New York Times prints two full pages of people Trump has insulted. Trump justifies sexual assault by saying "I'm sure she's never been grabbed before." Yuuuuuge new revelations include nobody remembering why Trump took $17 million in hurricane aid, associates of Trump's campaign saying they'd be willing to take illegal $2 million donation from China, and Breitbart's coordination with liberal activist "disrupting" GOP events.

October 25, 2016
In continued battle with party leaders, Trump cuts off fundraising for Republicans.

October 26, 2016
Trump spends the day in the final 2-week stretch advertising his new hotel in non-battleground Washington, D.C. Trump's 12th sexual assault accuser comes forward -- former Miss Finland.

October 27, 2016
Trump continues to say states like Republican-controlled Texas are rigging votes, while he suggests "We should just cancel the election and just give it to Trump." Trump's Victory Fund raises a paltry amount for the RNC in the crucial month of October. "Shark Tank" star Barbara Corcoran accuses Trump of inspecting her breasts.

October 28, 2016
In a bizarre turn of events, Carlos Danger resurfaces to disrupt the Clinton campaign. Meanwhile, Trump adds $10 million of his own money to support campaign advertising (after only spending $33,000 of his own money in the first 19 days of October.) Additionally, video footage of another highly creepy Miss Universe incident surfaces.

October 29, 2016
In the latest display of antisemitism, totally-not-deplorable Trump supporter chants "JEW-S-A" at rally. Meanwhile, a Trump supporter is arrested in Iowa while attempting to vote for a second time in an effort to un-rig the election. David Fahrenthold releases comprehensive list of Trump's charitable donations, which shows that Trump's claims do not add up.

October 30, 2016
Trump's possible ties to Russia resurface as Democrats call on the FBI to disclose details of suspected probe. Trump makes preposterous claim that Clinton will let 650 million new immigrants into U.S.

October 31, 2016
Trump's tax woes continue as The New York Times details how his tax-dodging methods might be dodgier than initially reported. Trump's Russia woes continue as NBC reports that the FBI is making an inquiry into his former campaign manager. Trump spends Halloween campaigning in solidly blue states. Report shows Trump's companies destroyed emails in defiance of court orders. Trump refuses to pay pollster $750,000.

November 1, 2016
Trump continues to hammer Clinton email probe as "absolutely devastating" with no evidence or public info released by FBI.

November 2, 2016
Trump tells his supporters to "pretend" that they're behind. Trump child rape accuser cancels press conference due to death threats.

November 3, 2016
Trump continues to push debunked Clinton Foundation indictment story from Fox. Rudy Guiliani openly flaunts that the Trump campaign received leaked FBI intel. Trump announces uncharacteristically small, invitation-only event for Election Night meltdown.

November 4, 2016
100 days have passed since the last Trump press conference. Newsweek shows Trump campaign's deep connection to Russia. Ohio judge orders Trump campaign to stop intimidating voters. Melania Trump revealed to have worked in USA prior to obtaining legal permission.

November 5, 2016
Early voting in Nevada polls show Trump getting trounced. Trump cancels Wisconsin rally just as Paul Ryan says he'll be there. The Wall Street Journal reports more details in Trump affair story which the National Enquirer paid to suppress.

November 6, 2016
Bloomberg reports on the disastrous effect a Trump victory could have on the value of the dollar. A New York Times profile on Trump in the final days of his candidacy paints him as an insecure, nervous wreck who can't sleep. Trump loses his twitter privileges.

November 7, 2016
Trump voters just can't stop committing voter fraud. Trump officially breaks 40 years of tradition by not releasing tax returns. Tom Brady and Gisele deny voting for Trump.
